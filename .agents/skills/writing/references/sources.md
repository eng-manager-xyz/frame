# Writing skill research sources

Use this reference to audit or update the skill. The runtime instructions keep
only the decisions that affect writing behavior.

- [OpenAI, “Prompting guidance for GPT-5.6 Sol”](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/guides/prompt-guidance-gpt-5p6)
  supports outcome-first instructions, explicit preservation priorities,
  decision rules instead of needless absolutes, lean prompts, clear stopping
  conditions, and representative evaluation.
- [OpenAI, “Build skills”](https://learn.chatgpt.com/docs/build-skills)
  supports a concise trigger description, instruction-first design,
  progressive disclosure, and representative forward tests.
- [George Orwell, “Politics and the English Language”](https://www.orwellfoundation.com/the-orwell-foundation/orwell/essays-and-other-works/politics-and-the-english-language/)
  supplies the six original prose rules represented here by rules 1–5 and 21.
- [Harry Dry on *How I Write*, “Learn Great Copywriting in 76 Minutes”](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/learn-great-copywriting-in-76-minutes-harry-dry/id1700171470?i=1000662505479)
  supplies the three-question test for persuasive copy.
- [Harry Dry, “17 tips for great copywriting”](https://marketingexamples.com/copywriting/tips)
  supports cutting clutter while preserving personality, scannability, stories,
  and conversational rhythm.
- [Digital.gov, “Principles of plain language”](https://digital.gov/guides/plain-language/principles)
  makes audience the first plain-language decision and supports topic sentences,
  useful organization, active voice, lists, and tables.
- [Google developer documentation style guide, “Active voice”](https://developers.google.com/style/voice)
  documents valid passive-voice cases: object emphasis, actor de-emphasis, and
  situations where the actor does not matter.
- [Google developer documentation style guide, “Avoid excessive claims”](https://developers.google.com/style/excessive-claims)
  supports objective, durable wording and sourced performance or security claims.
- [Microsoft Writing Style Guide, “Use simple words, concise sentences”](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/style-guide/word-choice/use-simple-words-concise-sentences)
  supports familiar precise words, concise sentences, and stable terminology
  without forcing abrupt or unfriendly prose.
- [FTC Policy Statement Regarding Advertising Substantiation](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/ftc-policy-statement-regarding-advertising-substantiation)
  requires a reasonable basis for express and implied objective advertising
  claims before dissemination; applicable legal review still controls.
